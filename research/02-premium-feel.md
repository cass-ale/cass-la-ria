# What Makes a Website Feel Premium — Practitioner Consensus

## Source: r/web_design thread (March 2026, 57 upvotes)
URL: https://www.reddit.com/r/web_design/comments/1rq6hkq/what_makes_a_website_feel_expensive/

## Top-voted insights (by community consensus):

### 1. Assets are #1 (21 upvotes, atalkingfish)
"The #1 most important thing that makes a website look expensive is assets. High quality photos and videos that are specific to the website owner (ie, not stock footage)."
"Premium appearance in websites is all down to assets... the biggest and best websites are 20% coding and design and 80% asset featuring."
"Signaling to others that you are premium always works on this principle: do what is difficult for others to do."

### 2. Details and restraint (153 upvotes, Sad-Salt24)
"Strong typography, consistent spacing, high-quality imagery, and subtle animations that feel smooth rather than flashy. Clear hierarchy, fast load times, and thoughtful interactions (like hover states or transitions)."

### 3. Basics over trends (44 upvotes, individyouall)
"Good design. Good experience. Barely anything that resembles current trends, instead a focus on getting the basics right: typography and imagery. And white space. Lots of white space. And absolutely no hard sell marketing crap."

### 4. Speed (4 upvotes, tamingunicorn)
"Speed. Every expensive-feeling site I've used loads instantly. The moment there's a layout shift or loading spinner the illusion breaks."

### 5. Responsive care (4 upvotes, Porsche924)
"Actual care in the responsive design. So not just that stuff changes on mobile, but you can resize the window and nothing jumps out as the wrong size."

### 6. No ads/popups (19 upvotes, labanjohnson)
Clean, uninterrupted experience.

### 7. Consistency (2 upvotes, trashbytes)
"For me it's consistency in every aspect. And the right amount of everything."

### 8. Restraint and confidence (equivalent_bird_6498)
"An 'expensive' website often comes from restraint and confidence in design. It's about knowing what to show and what to leave out, using space and pacing deliberately, and making every element feel purposeful."

### 9. Custom typography (2 upvotes, sxeros)
"Custom font types" — unique typefaces signal investment.

### 10. Minimal JS can still feel luxurious (qqqqqx)
"Some completely static pages with zero clientside JS can feel luxurious when designed right. In fact I'd say if not extremely tastefully those flashy clientside animations can take more away from the luxury factor instead of adding to it."
