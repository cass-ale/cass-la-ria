# Creative/Musician Website Best Practices Research

## Source: Webflow - "23 portfolio website examples, plus best practices" (Oct 2025)
URL: https://webflow.com/blog/design-portfolio-examples

Key portfolio best practices:
1. Curate diverse work — show range across mediums
2. Prioritize quality over quantity — every piece should be your best
3. Provide context and insights — storytelling behind each project
4. Update regularly — keep content fresh with dates
5. Demonstrate results and impact — quantifiable metrics
6. Express your personality — cohesive working relationship signal

## Source: Hypebot - "10 Great Musician Websites" (Dec 2024)
URL: https://www.hypebot.com/10-great-band-and-musician-websites/

Key patterns from successful musician websites:
1. Artist name front and center when site loads
2. Social media links clear and inviting
3. Menu subtle but accessible
4. Cohesive color palette echoing header imagery
5. EPK (Electronic Press Kit) page with: bio, music samples, press quotes, photos, videos, contact form
6. Sitewide music player for seamless streaming
7. One-page format with section navigation (for simpler sites)
8. Professional logo/branding
9. Dynamic header images showing musical range
10. Classy, minimal menu items (Home, Events, EPK)

## Source: CD Baby DIY Musician - EPK Checklist (Aug 2025)
URL: https://diymusician.cdbaby.com/music-marketing/epk-checklist/

Essential EPK elements for brand/press attraction:
1. Artist bio (short + long versions)
2. High-quality promotional photos (downloadable)
3. Music samples/embeds
4. Video content
5. Press coverage and reviews
6. Notable achievements/highlights
7. Contact information
8. Social media links with follower counts
9. Technical rider/stage plot (for live performers)

## Source: Link in Bio vs Full Website debate
URL: https://blog.liinks.co/link-in-bio-vs-full-website-the-seo-tradeoffs-no-ones-talking-a

Key insight: A link-in-bio page (like Linktree) works as a testing ground, but a full website gives more room for storytelling, SEO, and professional credibility. The current Cass la Ria site functions essentially as a link-in-bio page — it's a single-page hub with social links and contact. This is valid but limits brand attraction potential.

## What brands look for in a creative's website:
1. Professional presentation (signals reliability)
2. Content portfolio (proof of creative quality)
3. Audience metrics (follower counts, engagement rates)
4. Clear contact/booking information
5. Previous brand collaborations (social proof)
6. Media kit / EPK availability
7. Consistent visual identity across platforms

## Source: Reddit r/influencermarketing - "What brands actually look for in collabs in 2025"
URL: https://www.reddit.com/r/influencermarketing/comments/1mzqm0m/what_brands_actually_look_for_in_collabs_in_2025/

Brand evaluation criteria:
1. Audience fit (ICP) — country split, age/gender, language, interests
2. Content quality — clean, clear, visually appealing
3. Niche clarity — can a brand immediately understand what you create?
4. Engagement rates over raw follower counts
5. Authenticity — genuine recommendations
6. Professional presentation — signals reliability

## Source: Impact.com - "What do brands look for in influencers today?"
URL: https://impact.com/influencer/what-do-brands-look-for-in-influencers-relationship-building-strategies/

Key insight: Creators should use communication approaches that make partnership effortless for brand teams. This includes sharing timely updates, asking thoughtful questions, and having a professional online presence that demonstrates capability.
