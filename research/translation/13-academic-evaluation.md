# Academic Evaluation of Literary AI Translation

## Source: LITRANSPROQA Framework (Zhang et al., May 2025)
URL: https://slator.com/qa-based-evaluation-takes-on-literary-ai-translation/
Universities of Mannheim, Aberdeen, Ghent, and Nuremberg

### What It Is:
A QA-based evaluation framework that uses LLMs to assess literary translations by answering 25 targeted questions sourced from literary translation theory, translator interviews, and training materials.

### Key Evaluation Dimensions (from 25 expert-verified questions):
1. Does the translation capture the author's **tone**?
2. Does it handle **cultural references** well?
3. Is the **style** preserved?
4. Are **figurative expressions** handled appropriately?
5. Is the **register** (formality level) consistent with the original?
6. Are **ambiguities** preserved where intended?
7. Does the translation maintain **rhythm and flow**?
8. Are **proper nouns** handled consistently?
9. Is the **emotional impact** preserved?
10. Does it read naturally in the target language (**fluency**)?

### Critical Finding:
LITRANSPROQA was "particularly effective in **distinguishing professional human translations from AI translations**" — meaning there ARE measurable differences between human and AI literary translation that experts can detect.

### Implication for Our Skill:
These 10 dimensions should be our self-evaluation checklist. After every translation, we should verify each dimension. This is what separates professional literary translation from AI output.

## Synthesis: The 5 Quality Dimensions Our Skill Must Address

Based on all research (Nature study, GPT-4 benchmarking, community critiques, LITRANSPROQA):

### 1. VOICE (the #1 gap in AI translation)
- AI flattens voice into "middle register"
- Community: "AI has a voice — it requires a lot of editing to stifle that voice"
- Academic: LITRANSPROQA specifically tests for tone and style preservation
- **Our skill must**: Explicitly instruct to vary register, break uniformity, inject author personality

### 2. TEXTURE (rhythm, sentence variety, roughness)
- AI produces uniform sentence lengths and rhythms
- Community: "Every sentence ends up roughly the same length, same rhythm"
- Academic: Nature study found AI scored lowest on "poetic beauty" (5.0/10)
- **Our skill must**: Include anti-uniformity directives, mandate rhythm variation

### 3. CULTURAL RESONANCE (not just words but worlds)
- AI translates words but misses cultural weight
- Community: "The rhythms, the double meanings, even the cultural context"
- Academic: LITRANSPROQA tests cultural reference handling
- **Our skill must**: Include per-language cultural context guidance

### 4. EMOTIONAL FIDELITY (preserving the force)
- AI preserves meaning but loses emotional impact
- Community: "The emotion doesn't survive the translation"
- Academic: GPT-4 rated "junior-level" for emotional nuance
- **Our skill must**: Prioritize emotional effect over literal accuracy (Nida's dynamic equivalence)

### 5. LEXICAL CONSISTENCY (terminology coherence)
- AI uses different words for the same concept across a document
- Academic: GPT-4's #2 weakness per Yan et al. (2024)
- **Our skill must**: Include terminology locking for brand terms and key concepts
