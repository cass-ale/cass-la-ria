# Wikipedia: Signs of AI Writing — Key Tells to Avoid in Translation
URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Signs_of_AI_writing

## Core Principle:
LLMs "regress to the mean" — they replace specific, unusual, nuanced facts with generic, positive descriptions. Text becomes "simultaneously less specific and more exaggerated."

## AI-ism Vocabulary to NEVER Use in Translations:
### Legacy/Significance Words:
- stands/serves as, is a testament/reminder
- vital/significant/crucial/pivotal/key role/moment
- underscores/highlights its importance/significance
- reflects broader, symbolizing its ongoing/enduring/lasting
- contributing to the, setting the stage for
- marking/shaping the, represents/marks a shift
- key turning point, evolving landscape, focal point
- indelible mark, deeply rooted

### Filler/Hedging Words (from Forbes/Medium analysis):
- delve, tapestry, intricate, foster, nuanced
- landscape, realm, multifaceted, embark
- leverage, navigate, robust, comprehensive
- furthermore, moreover, additionally, in conclusion
- it's important to note, it's worth mentioning
- in today's world, at its core

### Structural Tells:
1. **Rule of Three** — AI loves listing exactly 3 things
2. **Parallel Structure** — every sentence mirrors the previous one
3. **Uniform Sentence Length** — no short punchy sentences, no long flowing ones
4. **Over-use of em dashes** — AI uses — constantly
5. **Title Case overuse** — capitalizing words that shouldn't be
6. **Excessive boldface** — bolding too many terms
7. **Inline vertical lists** — turning prose into bullet points unnecessarily

## The 12 Anti-AI-Ism Rules for Our Translation Skill:

1. **Never use "testament," "tapestry," "delve," "landscape," "foster," "embark," "leverage," "navigate," "robust," "nuanced," "multifaceted," "intricate"** in any translation
2. **Vary sentence length dramatically** — 3-word sentences next to 30-word sentences
3. **Break parallel structure** — if one sentence is Subject-Verb-Object, the next should NOT be
4. **Use contractions** where the target language allows informal register
5. **Prefer specific over generic** — "the third step on the left" not "a significant location"
6. **Limit em dashes** — max 1 per 500 words
7. **Never list exactly 3 things** unless the source does
8. **Avoid hedging preambles** — "It's worth noting that" → just say it
9. **Use unexpected word choices** — the word that's 80% right but 100% interesting
10. **Vary register within a piece** — mix formal and casual
11. **Let some sentences be grammatically imperfect** — fragments, run-ons, ellipses
12. **Never end with a summary that restates what was just said**
