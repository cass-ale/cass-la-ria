# UX Visual Design Principles Research

## Source: NNGroup - "5 Principles of Visual Design in UX" (Kelley Gordon, March 2020)
URL: https://www.nngroup.com/articles/principles-visual-design/

### 1. Scale
- Using relative size to signal importance and rank
- No more than 3 different sizes for a visually pleasing design
- Biggest elements = most important (noticed first)
- Creates variety and establishes visual hierarchy

### 2. Visual Hierarchy
- Guiding the eye through design elements in order of importance
- Implemented via scale, value, color, spacing, placement
- Use 2-3 typeface sizes to indicate content importance
- Bright colors for important items, muted for less important
- Include small, medium, and large components

### 3. Balance
- Equally distributed (not necessarily symmetrical) visual signal
- Types: Symmetrical (quiet, static), Asymmetrical (dynamic, energetic), Radial
- No one area should draw eye so much you can't see other areas
- Asymmetry creates energy and movement — fitting for creative brands

### 4. Contrast
- Juxtaposition of visually dissimilar elements to show they're different
- Applied through color, size, shape
- Critical for accessibility (WCAG contrast ratios)
- Makes CTAs and important elements stand out

### 5. Gestalt Principles
- How humans perceive visual elements as groups
- Proximity: elements close together are perceived as related
- Similarity: similar elements are perceived as related
- Common region: elements within a boundary are grouped
- Continuity: eye follows lines and curves
- Closure: brain fills in missing parts

## Key Takeaway
"Graphics that take advantage of the principles of good visual design can drive engagement and increase usability."
