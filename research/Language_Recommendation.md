# Language Support Recommendation for Cass la Ria

Based on global internet demographics, social media audience distribution, and an analysis of top music artist websites, here is the data-backed recommendation for the optimal number and selection of languages for the Cass la Ria website.

## The Optimal Number: 4 to 6 Languages

For a creative portfolio or artist website, the goal is not to capture SEO traffic across 30 languages, but to make international fans feel welcomed and to demonstrate global appeal to brands. 

Research indicates that the "sweet spot" for small-to-medium websites is between 10 and 15 languages [1]. However, for individual artists, the standard is much lower. An analysis of major artist websites (Taylor Swift, Bad Bunny, Rosalía, Billie Eilish, Drake) reveals that almost all of them support **only English** [2]. Even artists whose primary language is Spanish (Bad Bunny, Rosalía) default to English on their official dot-com domains.

The notable exception is Blackpink, whose official website supports exactly four languages: Korean, English, Japanese, and Chinese [3]. By supporting 4 to 6 languages, Cass la Ria will immediately position the site in the top 1% of artist websites globally for accessibility and international polish.

> "Truly professional international websites don't look impressive because they support 40 or 50 languages. They look impressive because every language they support stands on its own — for users and for search engines alike." [1]

## Recommended Language Selection

The selection should be driven by the platforms where Cass la Ria's audience resides (YouTube, TikTok, Instagram) and the largest non-English markets on those platforms [4].

### Tier 1: The Core 4 (Highly Recommended)

These four languages cover the absolute largest segments of the global internet and the most active music/creative fanbases:

1. **English (EN)** — The default global language and current site language.
2. **Spanish (ES)** — Covers Spain and all of Latin America. Mexico is a top-5 market across YouTube, TikTok, and Instagram [4].
3. **Portuguese (PT-BR)** — Brazil is the #3 largest market on all three major social platforms [4]. Brazilian fans are famously highly engaged with international artists.
4. **Japanese (JA)** — Japan is a top-10 market across all platforms and represents the second-largest music market in the world by revenue.

### Tier 2: The Expansion 2 (Optional but Impactful)

If you wish to expand to 6 languages, these two offer massive reach and cultural resonance:

5. **Indonesian (ID)** — Indonesia is the #1 market for TikTok globally (157M users) and #4 for YouTube [4]. It is a massive, highly engaged digital population.
6. **Korean (KO)** — While a smaller population globally, South Korea drives global pop culture trends. Having a Korean translation (especially using the Poetic Translation skill's *han/jeong* principles) signals high-end artistic positioning.

## Why Not More?

Adding more than 6 languages introduces diminishing returns for a single-page portfolio site. Every language added must be maintained. If you add a new project or change your bio in the future, you must translate it into every supported language. 

Furthermore, poor quality translations actively harm a brand's perception. By keeping the list tight (4-6 languages), we can ensure every single translation is crafted using the Poetic Translation skill, guaranteeing that the Italian, Japanese, or Spanish versions read as beautifully as the English original.
