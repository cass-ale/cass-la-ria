# Rain Quality Grading Criteria — Rock Paper Shotgun
Source: https://www.rockpapershotgun.com/an-extremely-thorough-grading-of-the-best-rain-in-video-games

## Professional Grading Criteria (6 dimensions)
1. **Wetness** — Do surfaces look wet? Does rain interact visibly with objects?
2. **Intensity** — How strong/variable is the rain? Sheets vs drizzle?
3. **Petrichor** — Does it FEEL like being in rain? Immersion factor.
4. **Etherealness** — Wind effects, depth, irregularity, change over time.
5. **Restfulness** — Mood/atmosphere contribution.
6. **Sound** — Audio quality of rain effects.

## Key Insights from Top-Rated Games

### Batman: Arkham Knight (Wetness: 10/10)
"When Batman splays out his cape to glide and all the rain is wicked off the fabric?
That's the exact moment Arkham Knight maxes out the wetness meter."
- Rain interacting with objects (cape, surfaces) is THE highest-rated quality
- "Slick, shiny textures is one thing that Arkham Knight does better than pretty much any other game"

### Arkham Knight (Etherealness: 9/10)
"Look closely at the droplets and you'll see that they're all angled slightly differently.
There's depth, there's irregularity, there's ever-changing direction."
- Per-drop angle variation = high etherealness score
- Depth layers with different angles = professional quality

### The Cycle: Frontier (Etherealness: 8/10)
"The wind effects are great. The rain could definitely follow the wind a bit more,
but on the whole there's a lot of very good change over time in the atmosphere,
the strength, and the angle of the rain. It feels surprisingly lifelike."
- Wind variation over time is critical for realism
- Rain angle should follow wind direction

### The Cycle: Frontier (Wetness: 5/10)
"There's a nice little splash effect when each droplet hits the ground, but look closer
and you'll see that the ground textures more or less stay the same."
- Splash alone isn't enough — the SURFACE needs to react too
- For our umbrella: the umbrella itself should show impact feedback

## Application to Our Umbrella Collision
From these professional criteria, our collision needs:
1. **Visible impact feedback** on the umbrella surface (not just deflection)
2. **Per-drop angle variation** in the deflection (not uniform)
3. **Depth and irregularity** in how drops interact with the curve
4. **Wind-responsive deflection** — deflection angle should follow current wind
